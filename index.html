Greek Mythology

Greece was the foundation of Roman society, and many aspects of Roman life were influenced by Greek culture, including their mythology! Because of this, you will find many similarities between their mythologies. (Mostly only the names of the gods will be different!) I hope that you learn something from this section!

As you can see from the picture above, the family tree of Greek mythology is extremely complicated. First off, everything came from the void of Chaos, who gave birth to 5 deities, one of them being Gaea, the goddess of the Earth. Gaea gave birth to children on her own, then married one of her sons, Ouranus, and bore the Titans, Gigantes, and the Cyclopes. Then, Cronus took over as the ruler of the universe, and married his sister Rhea, and she bore the first generation of the Olympians, Hades, god of the dead/Underworld, Demeter, goddess of farming, Hestia, goddess of the hearth, Poseidon, god of the sea, Hera, goddess of marriage, and, of course, Zeus, god of the sky. Rhea hides Zeus, while the rest of his siblings are swallowed by Cronus, who was afraid that his power will be taken from him. In the end, Zeus and the Olympians won the battle against the Titans, along with the Cyclopes and the Hecatoncheires, another one of Gaea's children. After winning the battle, the gods married and gave birth amongst themselves, as shown in the picture. Gaea was furious at Zeus for imprisoning the Titans, since they were her children as well, so she unleashed the monsters Typhon and Echidna, who were the father and mother of all monsters. Zeus eventually won over them, and there was peace on Mount Olympus, where the gods lived, for some time. The gods and goddesses were immortal, with ichor that flowed through their veins, and they ate ambrosia and nectar as food. Heroes also existed in Greek mythology, some that you may already know, including Heracles (more commonly known as Hercules), Perseus, and Theseus. There were also many other monsters in Greek mythology, including Scylla, Charybdis, harpies, Hydra, the Nemean Lion, Ladon, the Chiimera, the Sphinx, Python, and so many more! This paragraph was just a brief overview of Greek mythology. Keep in mind to research more on the topic if you are interested, and you should probably also study the image, just so you know the family tree of the Greek gods!

Roman Mythology

Like I mentioned earlier, Roman mythology was based largely on Greek mythology. The Romans made their own system of gods, with different personalities and names, but they were the deities of the same things. For example, Zeus became Jupiter, and Poseidon became Neptune. (You may have also realized that our planets are named after Roman gods!)

Honestly, this paragraph is basically the same as the Greek mythology summary, since only the names have changed. Terra and her siblings were made from Chaos, and she married Uranus, her son, and gave birth to the Titans, Cyclopes, and the Hecatoncheires, as well as others. Terra plotted against Uranus, because he imprisoned some of their children in Tartarus, the pit. Saturn, one of the Titans, ruled the universe through the Golden Ages. Ops, his wife, gave birth to six children, Pluto, god of the Underworld/dead, Ceres, goddess of farming, Neptune, god of the sea, Vesta, goddess of the hearth, Juno, goddess of marriage, and Jupiter, god of the sky, and he swallowed them all, except for the last child, Jupiter, which Ops hides through her mother, Terra's instructions. Jupiter grew up and overthrew Saturn through a fierce battle, in which he freed his siblings, and Terra's other children. Jupiter ruled over the world after he imprisoned the Titans in Tartarus. Jupiter had many children with other goddesses, as well as mortals. The other gods had many children as well, and the Olympians ruled over the world. Like the Greek gods and goddesses, the Roman deities also had ichor in their veins, and ate ambrosia and nectar. There were also beasts in Roman mythology, but with different names. For example, satyrs were fauns, the Furies were the Dirae, and so on so forth. Keep in mind that this family tree might not be the full family tree of the Roman gods, so feel free to research on your own!

Norse Mythology

Norse mythology is a bit different than the other mythologies, particularly because the Norse gods weren't immortal, and could be killed. The Norse people had something of a doomsday in their mythology, known as Ragnarök, in which all of the monsters and enemies of the gods would destroy them, and take over the world. Odin was considered the father of the Æsir gods, a pantheon of gods, and his sons and daughters became part of the Æsir, mostly "warrior" gods, that included Thor, Tyr, Baldur, Heimdall, and more.He was the husband of Frigg, and lived in the realm of Asgard, above Midgard, the land of the mortals. There were also the Vanir, gods and goddesses that represented fertility, health, wisdom, and the ability to see the future. The most famous, in my opinion, of the Vanir, were Frey, god of peace, prosperity, and other similar aspects, and Freyja, his sister, goddess of beauty, similar to Aphrodite, in a way. The gods' main enemies were giants in the realm of Jötunheim. That's why Odin made a peace treaty with the giants, and raised one of their children, Loki, as a "god" in Asgard. Thor and Loki get into many conflicts, but they make up eventually, until Loki kills Baldur, and is punshed for his crime. Loki's children are mostly monsters that lead to Ragnarök, including Fenrir, Jormungandr, and Hel, goddess of the dead. After Ragnarök happens, all of the gods die except for one, Vidar the Silent, one of Odin's sons. He rules the next generation of the Norse gods.

Egyptian Mythology

Nun was the first god/goddess in Egyptian mythology, much like Chaos in Greek mythology. Her, or his, children were Khnum and Nit, who married to have children, one of them being Ra, the god of the Sun, who became the king of the gods. He made Shu and Tefnut, god of the wind, and goddess of rain, respectively. They had children together, Geb and Nut, who were the god of the Earth, and goddess of the sky, respectively. (More like the opposite of Greek mythology!) They had children, even though Ra forbade them to, so they were punished by being separated for the rest of their "lives". Their children were Set, Nephthys, Osiris, Isis, and Horus. Osiris became king after Ra, from Isis's trickery and cunning. Set became jealous of Osiris, and he plotted against him. At a banquet that he organized, he trapped Osiris in a beautiful coffin. Isis, Osiris's wife, spent a long time searching for the coffin with her sister, Nephthys, the wife of Set, who agreed to help her. Isis became pregnant with Horus, in a different life, and he avenged his father's death by defeating Set and exiling him to the desert. There were also many other gods and goddesses, including Tawaret, goddess of pregnancy, Bastet, goddess of cats, Anubis, god of the dead (who is not pictured), Ammit, the devourer of souls, etc. Monsters in Egyptian mythology included griffins, serpopards, the Bennu (very similar to the phoenix), and many more!
